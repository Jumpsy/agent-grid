# Business-genre design catalog — how to design for ANY type of business

The Avengers must be able to design a premium, on-genre site for **any** business, not just dark
AI-SaaS. The #1 way a site reads as templated/AI-slop is **wrong genre** — a dark gradient product
page for a bakery, or a playful mascot site for a law firm. So: **identify the genre first, then
build to its DNA.** This catalog gives the spine for the major archetypes; the method at the bottom
derives the DNA for anything not listed. Every genre still inherits the universal spine (below).

## Universal spine (applies to EVERY genre, always)
Honesty (zero fabricated stats/reviews/avatars), **no dashes anywhere** (use "to"/comma/period),
humanizer pass on all copy, real icons (Iconify/Lucide/simple-icons, never emojis, never AI-vibe
glyphs, and NO box/tile around the icon by default per SKILL.md rule 15), real photos
saved into the project (§2.5 pipeline) instead of gray boxes, **photoreal PEOPLE generated and placed
where a person belongs** (the worker doing the job, real users on the app, diners/members/students/
guests per genre, per §2.5's people-imagery rule) so the site never feels cold or person-less,
deliberate palette where every color
earns its place (§5), psychology-led structure (§5.5: pain→solution→result, one big promise, trust
stacking, friction removal, clear 3-second answer), full SEO suite, a11y, mobile-first (375 + 1440),
real working links, polished loading/empty/error states, and **verify with screenshots before done**.
What changes per genre: **theme, hero, palette mood, section order, conversion target, proof type.**

## The genre router — pick by what the business IS
The single question that decides theme + hero: **"is the star a software product the user operates,
or a real-world thing they book / buy / visit / trust?"** Then match the archetype:

### 1. AI / SaaS / dev tool / tech product  →  see §0.5 (the dark-product default)
- **Theme:** dark by default (premium tech). **Hero:** split-screen, tight benefit headline + ONE
  CTA + honest proof + a LIVE animated product demo. **Proof:** the product itself + integration marquee.
- **Sections:** nav → demo hero → logo/social proof → features (bento) → integrations marquee →
  pricing cards (equal sizes) → FAQ → final CTA → footer. **Conversion:** sign-up / start-free.
- **Palette:** near-black/charcoal base, one signature accent, restrained. **Watch:** no fake metrics,
  no sparkle glyphs, no bobbing mock.

### 2. Local service / trades / home & property  →  see `local-service-business.md` (deep dive)
- Plumber, roofer, paver, HVAC, electrician, landscaper, cleaner, pest control, tree service, mover.
- **Theme:** LIGHT + warm. **Hero:** benefit headline (one word in brand color) + real before/after
  photo of the work + a sticky quote form (Name/Email/Phone/Address). **Proof:** the work, licensed
  & insured, reviews, supplier logos, a friendly mascot. **Conversion:** free-estimate form + phone call.
- **Palette:** one bold warm signature (gold/amber/red) + calm neutral ground + ONE action color.
- **Sections:** sticky estimate bar → hero → services grid → benefit cards → testimonials → trust
  strip → value-prop split (crew photo) → service-area table → process timeline → mascot/3-steps →
  sectors grid → loud phone CTA → supplier logos → brand-color footer with day-by-day hours.

### 3. Restaurant / café / bar / bakery / food truck
- **Theme:** warm, appetite-driven, often dark-moody OR bright-fresh per cuisine. **Hero:** full-bleed
  mouth-watering food/space photography + the name + one line of vibe + "Reserve" / "Order online" /
  "View menu". **Proof:** the food photos, ambiance, real reviews, awards. **Conversion:** reservation
  (Resy/OpenTable), online order, or "find us". **Must-haves:** the MENU (real items + prices),
  hours + map + parking, photo gallery, story/chef, reservations, events/private-dining, social feed.
- **Palette:** evocative of the cuisine (terracotta/cream for Italian, deep green/brass for
  upscale, neon/warm for diner). **Watch:** food photos must look real and delicious, never stocky;
  prices use "$" not dashes; hours "12pm to 10pm".

### 4. Health / medical / dental / clinic / therapy / veterinary
- **Theme:** light, calm, clean, reassuring. **Hero:** warm photo of provider/care + a calming
  headline focused on the patient outcome + "Book an appointment" / "New patients welcome".
- **Palette:** soft blues/teals/greens, lots of white, gentle. **Proof:** credentials, board
  certifications, years, real (anonymized-or-consented) testimonials, insurance accepted, before/after
  only where ethical. **Sections:** services/treatments, conditions, meet-the-team (real bios + photos),
  insurance/payment, new-patient info, location/hours, book. **Conversion:** appointment booking + phone.
- **Watch:** never fabricate outcomes or reviews (regulated); accessibility is critical; calm not flashy.

### 5. Fitness / gym / studio / personal trainer / coach
- **Theme:** high-energy, bold, often dark with a punchy accent; strong motion. **Hero:** dynamic
  action photo/video of training + bold transformation/identity headline + "Start free trial" /
  "Book a class". **Proof:** real member transformations (consented), class schedule, trainer cred.
- **Palette:** dark base + one electric accent (lime, orange, red). **Sections:** programs/classes,
  schedule, membership pricing tiers, trainers, transformations, free-trial CTA, location.
- **Conversion:** free trial / first class. **Watch:** real people only, motion purposeful not frantic.

### 6. Beauty / salon / spa / aesthetics / nails / barber
- **Theme:** elegant, editorial, serene (light & airy OR moody-luxe). **Hero:** beautiful result/space
  photography + refined headline + "Book now". **Palette:** soft neutrals, blush, sage, gold, or
  black-and-gold luxe. **Proof:** portfolio/gallery, before/after, reviews, stylist bios.
- **Sections:** service menu + prices, gallery, team, booking, products, gift cards. **Conversion:**
  online booking. **Watch:** type and spacing must feel premium/editorial; real portfolio, not stock.

### 7. Professional services / B2B / agency / consulting / law / accounting / finance-advisory
- **Theme:** authoritative, restrained, credible. Law/accounting/finance = conservative, trust-forward
  (deep navy, serif accents, lots of white). Creative/marketing agency = bolder, portfolio-led, more
  expressive. **Hero:** clear "what we do for whom + the outcome" headline + "Book a consultation" /
  "Get in touch" + a credibility row (clients, results, years). **Proof:** case studies (real, with
  numbers if true), client logos, team credentials, testimonials.
- **Sections:** services, case studies/work, approach/process, team, results, insights/blog, contact.
- **Conversion:** consultation/contact form. **Watch:** gravitas over gimmicks for law/finance;
  for agencies, the WORK is the hero — show it big.

### 8. Real estate / property / agent / brokerage
- **Theme:** aspirational, photography-led, clean and premium. **Hero:** stunning property/area photo
  + search or "What's your home worth?" + agent value line. **Proof:** listings, sold record,
  testimonials, local expertise, agent bio. **Sections:** listing search/grid, featured properties,
  neighborhoods, sell-your-home (valuation CTA), buyer/seller guides, agent profile, contact.
- **Conversion:** listing inquiry + home-valuation lead. **Watch:** real high-quality photos;
  honest sales record.

### 9. E-commerce / DTC product brand
- **Theme:** product-and-brand-led; theme follows the brand (clean minimal, or bold/playful, or luxe).
  **Hero:** the product, beautifully shot, + the one-line value + "Shop now" + trust (free shipping,
  returns, reviews). **Proof:** product photography, real reviews/ratings, UGC, press. **Sections:**
  collection/product grid, product detail (gallery, variants, reviews, add-to-cart), bestsellers,
  benefits/ingredients/specs, shipping & returns trust, story, newsletter. **Conversion:** add-to-cart
  / checkout. **Watch:** reviews must be real; crisp product imagery; fast; mobile-first checkout.

### 10. Creative / portfolio / personal brand / photographer / designer / artist / musician
- **Theme:** work-first, minimal chrome, distinctive type, big imagery, can be experimental. **Hero:**
  the work itself (a striking piece or a reel) + name + discipline + a subtle CTA. **Proof:** the
  portfolio. **Sections:** selected work/projects (big visuals, case detail), about, services/process,
  contact, maybe shop/press. **Conversion:** contact / hire / inquiry. **Watch:** let the work breathe;
  typography and layout craft carry it; avoid template grids — curate.

### 11. Education / course / coaching / info-product / school / bootcamp / tutoring
- **Theme:** outcome- and trust-driven, friendly-credible. **Hero:** the transformation/outcome
  headline + who it's for + "Enroll" / "Start free lesson" + proof. **Proof:** student outcomes
  (honest), curriculum, instructor authority, testimonials, accreditation. **Sections:** outcomes,
  curriculum/modules, instructor bio, testimonials/results, pricing/cohort dates, FAQ, enroll, guarantee.
- **Conversion:** enroll / waitlist / free-lesson lead. **Watch:** no fabricated success stats;
  specificity beats hype ("land a job in 6 months" only if true).

### 12. Events / conference / wedding / festival / venue
- **Theme:** immersive, date-and-place forward, energetic. **Hero:** event name + date + location +
  hero visual + "Get tickets" / "RSVP" + countdown. **Sections:** about, schedule/agenda,
  speakers/lineup/performers, venue + travel, tickets/pricing tiers, sponsors, FAQ. **Conversion:**
  ticket purchase / RSVP. **Watch:** the date/location must be impossible to miss; real lineup only.

### 13. Nonprofit / cause / community / church / charity
- **Theme:** mission-led, human, emotive but dignified. **Hero:** the mission in one line + a real
  human photo + "Donate" / "Volunteer" / "Join us". **Proof:** real impact (honest numbers), stories,
  transparency. **Sections:** mission, what-we-do/programs, impact (honest stats), stories, get
  involved (donate/volunteer/events), about/leadership, transparency/financials. **Conversion:**
  donate + volunteer signup. **Watch:** never inflate impact; real people and stories with consent.

### 14. Hospitality / hotel / resort / travel / tourism / vacation rental
- **Theme:** immersive, escapist, premium destination photography. **Hero:** breathtaking
  place/room photo (or video) + a sense-of-place headline + a booking widget (dates + guests +
  "Check availability"). **Proof:** photo galleries, reviews, amenities, location. **Sections:**
  rooms/suites, amenities, dining/experiences, gallery, location & getting-here, offers, book.
- **Conversion:** booking. **Watch:** real property photos; the booking widget is the focal point.

### 15. Finance / fintech / crypto / insurance / bank
- **Theme:** clean, secure, trustworthy; light-clean or dark-premium per brand. **Hero:** the clear
  money/benefit outcome + "Open account" / "Get a quote" + security/regulatory trust. **Proof:**
  security & compliance badges, real rates/terms, data viz, testimonials. **Sections:** product,
  rates/calculator, security & compliance, how-it-works, pricing/fees, FAQ, get-started. **Conversion:**
  account open / quote. **Watch:** regulated — accurate numbers/disclosures only; security forward;
  no hype crypto-slop.

### 16. Automotive / dealership / auto repair / detailing
- **Theme:** bold, vehicle-photography-led, trust for service. **Hero:** the vehicle/work + offer +
  "Browse inventory" / "Book service". **Sections:** inventory or services, financing/specials,
  about/reviews, book/contact, location. **Conversion:** inventory inquiry / service booking.

## Deriving a genre that isn't listed (so this truly covers EVERY business)
For anything not above, build the DNA from first principles in four steps:
1. **Classify the goal:** does the visitor need to **book, buy, call, visit, apply, donate, or
   sign up**? That single primary action = the conversion target the whole page funnels toward.
2. **Pick the proof type:** software → live demo; service → the work + credentials; product → the
   product photo + reviews; place → immersive photography; expertise → case studies/outcomes;
   cause → real stories + honest impact. The hero showcases the proof type.
3. **Set the emotional register → theme + palette:** premium-tech/nightlife/luxe → dark; care/health/
   clean/local-trade/editorial → light; appetite/energy → warm + bold. Then one signature brand color
   + a disciplined support palette (§5). Match the register of the real-world business, not a trend.
4. **Order sections by the buyer's questions** (§5.5): what is this & is it for me (hero) → can I
   trust you (proof) → how does it work / what do I get → handle objections → the one obvious CTA →
   footer. Every section must earn its place or it gets cut.

Then apply the universal spine and the relevant §0.5 / local-service spec as the nearest template.
When the genre is ambiguous or the brand could go two ways, ask ONE clarifying question
(register/theme or primary action) before building, then commit.

## Research-backed field notes (current as of 2026)
Concrete conventions + numbers from researching real high-converting sites per genre. Use these to
sharpen the archetypes above. (Numbers are industry findings to design BY, never to fabricate ON a
client's page.)

- **Restaurant/food:** Native HTML menu, never a PDF (PDFs are friction and feel dated). "Reserve a
  Table" / "Order Online" CTA above the fold. 70%+ traffic is mobile, so order completion (not
  prettiness) is the metric; load under 2s. Food photography is the engine: 43% of Gen Z pick by food
  photos, pro shots can lift a dish's orders ~30%. Story-first / destination feel for upscale; bright
  & fresh for casual. Exemplars to study: high-end restaurant sites on Framer/sitebuilderreport
  galleries. Must-haves: live menu+prices, hours+map+parking, gallery, reservations, private dining.
- **Health/medical/dental/vet:** Clean, calm, professional, reassuring. Real photos of the ACTUAL
  doctor/staff/office (not stock) are the top trust driver. Self-booking is now expected: 60%+ of
  patients prefer to book online, so a clear "Book Appointment" + click-to-call above the fold.
  Stack: credentials/awards, real testimonials, before/after where ethical, insurance/financing/
  membership + transparent costs, doctor intro video. Soft blue/teal/green + lots of white. A11y is
  critical (regulated audience). Never fabricate outcomes or reviews.
- **E-commerce/DTC:** Mobile drives 60-70% of traffic, build mobile-first then scale up; tap targets
  >=44px, ~40% less copy than desktop, persistent CTA. Load under 2-3s (53% bail past 3s). Feature the
  FOUNDER prominently (15-28% higher conversion vs faceless corporate, as AI-skepticism rose in
  2025-26). FUD-removing microcopy next to the CTA (free shipping, returns, guarantee). Guest checkout +
  Apple/Shop Pay (+20-30% mobile conv). Real reviews/UGC/press. Cart abandonment ~70% is the battle.
- **Law/professional/B2B:** Authority engine, not a brochure. 2026 look = "modern authority": cinematic
  photos of REAL people/offices (not stock), serif + sans pairing, bolder palettes creeping in (forest
  green, terracotta, navy + gold). Best firm sites "look like a well-designed SaaS product but feel like
  a 100-year institution." Trust via SPECIFIC verifiable badges (Chambers, Best Lawyers) over generic
  "top lawyer". KPI is lead QUALITY: pre-qualify via targeted practice-area content + clear intake.
  CTA: "Book a consultation". For creative agencies, flip to bold and let the WORK be the hero.
- **Real estate:** Lead with a real, placeable photo (a specific skyline/suburb/the agent's portrait),
  never stock house-and-keys. Benefit headline beats product ("Find Your Austin Home Before Anyone Else"
  > "Search Austin MLS"). Non-negotiables: "Book a Showing"/"Schedule a Call" above the fold, click-to-
  call on mobile, testimonial widget near the hero, "What's your home worth?" valuation lead magnet.
  Listings = responsive 16:9 card grid (photo, price, beds/baths/sqft, hover alt-image), 20+ photos +
  full-screen lightbox per listing. Clean, generous whitespace, photography-led.
- **Fitness/gym:** Answer "will I fit in here?" before price. Free-trial/guest-pass/"Book a Free Class"
  above the fold; stack 3 risk-reducers (free trial + guarantee + transparent pricing). 70%+ mobile;
  booking in <=3 steps. Dark backgrounds dominate (make photos pop, signal energy) + one energy accent
  (orange/red/electric blue); luxury = black + minimal accent, budget gyms = friendlier/brighter. Use
  REAL photos of the actual space/members/trainers. Sections: programs/classes, schedule, membership
  tiers, trainers, real transformations (consented), trial CTA.
- **Beauty/salon/spa:** Calm + elegant before the first visit. Soothing muted palette (blush, sage,
  nude, or black+gold luxe), elegant/script display font + clean body, lifestyle/result photography or
  a video background (cited ~138% conv lift). Online booking is non-negotiable (integrated booking =
  up to ~30% more appointments). Show the service menu WITH price tiers on-site, highlight popular
  packages. Sections: services+prices, gallery/before-after, team/stylists, booking, products, gift cards.
- **Hotel/hospitality/travel:** Immersive, escapist, large-format authentic photography (drone/aerial,
  360 tours); 20+ photos per room can lift bookings dramatically. The BOOKING WIDGET is the focal point:
  embed dates+guests+"Check Availability" in the header/hero, pre-filled (a visible pre-filled widget
  lifts engine CTR ~30%). "Cinematic conversion" = video walkthroughs to cut look-to-book friction.
  Sections: rooms/suites, amenities, dining/experiences, gallery, location+getting-here, offers, book.
- **Nonprofit/charity/church:** Lead with ONE person's story, not big stats (Identifiable Victim
  Effect: brains don't empathize with numbers). Emotional, dignified imagery + transparency (Charity:
  Water model: donor impact over org ego). "Rule of three" clicks homepage to thank-you. Donation form:
  preset amounts WITH impact context per amount, single-page, recurring-by-default. Trust badges
  (Charity Navigator/GuideStar/BBB) can lift donations ~15%. Honest animated impact dashboards. CTA:
  donate + volunteer.
- **Education/course/coaching:** Sell the before to after transformation, not a module list. Video
  testimonials convert 2-3x text; place proof above the fold AND after each objection. Instructor cred
  must be specific/current/relevant to the promise (recent work + teaching outcomes), not a generic bio.
  Offer 3-6 month payment plans (+20-40% conv) and a 30-90 day guarantee (longer = more confidence).
  Sections: outcomes, curriculum/modules, instructor, results/testimonials, pricing/cohort, FAQ, enroll.
- **Creative/portfolio/photographer:** Work-first, minimal chrome, generous whitespace, grid layouts,
  the work drawn to center stage. 2026: motion-first/interactive portfolios, micro-interaction
  navigation, living/regularly-updated galleries. Dark mode trending toward jewel tones (midnight blue,
  deep purple, racing green) over pure black. Distinctive functional type. CTA: hire/contact/inquiry.
- **Fintech/finance/insurance:** Trust + clarity + consistency. Show VISIBLE security evidence near
  forms (encryption/ISO 27001/SOC2 disclosures) — "your data is safe" text is not a signal. Clean
  minimalist layout, generous whitespace, strong hierarchy on the key numbers, limited color to guide
  decisions. Biometric/device auth where relevant. UX is conversion: good UX cited up to +200%; a 2-3s
  delay costs 20-30% of onboarding completions, a clunky KYC flow kills signups. CTA: open account / get
  a quote / get started. Calculators and clear rates/fees build confidence.

## Common slop patterns to avoid across ALL genres (from the research)
Stock house-and-keys / generic stock people / faceless corporate; PDF menus; walls of text; fake star
ratings or invented metrics; slow pages (>3s) and non-mobile-first layouts; burying the booking/quote/
donate CTA below the fold; multiple competing CTAs; default blue-to-purple SaaS gradient on a non-tech
brand; em/en dashes; emoji-as-icons; sparkle "AI magic" glyphs; **every icon wrapped in a rounded
tinted square/circle tile** (drop the box, put the icon straight on the card in the brand color);
hand-drawn/mismatched "AI flavor" icons instead of one real icon family; one lonely fade as the only
motion; gray placeholder boxes where a real photo belongs.
