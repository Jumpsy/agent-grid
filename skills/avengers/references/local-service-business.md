# Genre reference: Local service / trades business landing page

Extracted from a real, human-made site (a "Blacktopfix" asphalt-maintenance contractor page).
This is the **trust-driven, quote-capture** genre: the kind of site a marketing agency builds for a
plumber, roofer, paver, lawn-care crew, HVAC, electrician, cleaner, pest control, tree service, etc.
It is NOT the dark AI-SaaS-product genre the rest of the skill defaults to. When the client is a
local trade / home-or-property service / visually-driven local business, **build to THIS spec
instead of §0.5's dark-product spec.** The anti-slop, honesty, no-dashes, and quality bars still apply.

## Why this looks human (and converts), not AI-slop
- It is **light, warm, and approachable** — not a dark gradient tech page. Theme matches a working
  trade, not a startup.
- **Two real brand colors used with discipline**: one bold warm signature (gold/amber) + one calm
  neutral section ground (slate blue-gray), white cards, near-black text, and ONE secondary action
  color (a friendly blue) used sparingly. No purple SaaS gradient anywhere.
- **Trust is stacked everywhere**: licensed & insured, years in business, Google 5.0 + Facebook
  badges, real brand-partner logos (Graco, SealMaster, Sherwin-Williams), attributed testimonials,
  a before/after slider of the actual work, real photos of the real crew.
- **The whole page is a quote machine**: a sticky lead-capture bar (Name / Email / Phone / Address /
  GET ESTIMATE) rides under the nav on every screen, and the phone number is loud and repeated.
- **Local specificity**: an actual service-area table (counties → towns) and audience sectors
  (schools, HOAs, places of worship, clinics) — concrete, local, believable.
- **A friendly brand character** (a hard-hat gator mascot) + a hand-styled wordmark logo give it a
  human, small-business personality a template can't fake.

## Design tokens (starting palette — re-derive per client brand)
```
--brand:        #E0A82E   /* warm gold/amber — primary signature, used for the sticky form bar,
                             CTAs, highlighted headline words, the footer band */
--brand-ink:    #B07D14   /* darker gold for gold-on-light text/hover */
--ground:       #C7D2DC   /* calm slate blue-gray — the main alternating section background */
--ground-soft:  #E8EDF1   /* lighter wash for a second neutral band */
--paper:        #FFFFFF   /* cards */
--ink:          #1C2530   /* near-black body/heading text */
--ink-soft:     #5A6675   /* muted secondary text */
--action:       #5B9BD5   /* secondary friendly blue — audience/sector cards, step accents ONLY */
--success:      #2FA36B   /* check marks / "guaranteed" ticks, used sparingly */
```
- Hierarchy comes from **weight + the single gold highlight word**, not giant type (honor §0 rule 1:
  hero headline clamps ~2rem–3.25rem, restrained).
- Headline pattern: bold near-black phrase with ONE word swapped to gold —
  `Protect Your <gold>Asphalt</gold>. Protect Your <gold>Investment</gold>.`
- Fonts: one sturdy, slightly condensed display/heading sans (e.g. Archivo, Oswald-lite, Anton for
  the wordmark only) + one clean humanist body (e.g. Inter/Geist). Trade sites read warmer with a
  rounded-ish body than a cold geometric one.

## Section blueprint (order tells the conversion story)
1. **Sticky nav + persistent estimate bar** — logo + nav; directly under it a gold bar with the
   quick quote form (Name, Email, Phone, Address) + `GET ESTIMATE`. Stays visible while scrolling.
   Microcopy: "Most quotes delivered within 24 hours." This is the #1 conversion element — never drop it.
2. **Hero** — benefit-question headline ("Do You Have Tired Looking Pavement? Let's fix it."),
   a short plain-language paragraph of what you do + the promise (no jargon), and a **before/after
   image slider** of the real work. Real photo, not abstract art.
3. **Services grid** — "What Do We Do" — 3-column grid of service pills/cards, each a line icon +
   service name (Crack Repair, Line Striping, Sealcoating, Pressure Washing, ADA Stenciling…).
   Per §0 rule 18, put a RELEVANT real photo on the feature cards, not just icons.
4. **Benefit cards** — floating white cards with gold icons: "Extends Pavement Life", "Save
   Thousands Long-Term", "Improves Property Appearance", "Fast, Minimal Disruption". Outcomes, not features.
5. **Testimonials** — "Real Results. Real Property Owners." — attributed quote cards (name + role,
   e.g. "Michael R., Commercial Property Manager"). Use a quote mark, NOT fake star rows on
   placeholder reviews (§0 rule 18b). Only real, attributed proof.
6. **Trust strip** — inline ticks: "Fully Licensed & Insured · 14 Years Experience · Commercial &
   HOA Specialists", with a `GET A FREE, NO-PRESSURE ESTIMATE` button.
7. **Value-prop split** — "Protect Your Asphalt. Protect Your Investment." paragraph on the left,
   a **real photo of the crew/worker** on the right, with a small checklist overlay
   (Years Experience · Licensed & Insured · Satisfaction Guaranteed).
8. **Service-area table** — 2–3 columns grouping region → towns (e.g. county headers with city
   rows). Concrete local coverage = local-SEO + believability.
9. **Process timeline** — "Our Proven Process: Quick, Quality, Guaranteed." A vertical timeline with
   connected nodes and alternating cards (Free On-Site Evaluation → Surface Prep → Precision
   Application → …). Each node a labeled step.
10. **"3 Easy Steps" + brand mascot** — three colored step cards (Survey Location, Transparent
    Pricing, Set Expectations) beside a friendly brand mascot/character, plus Google 5.0 & Facebook
    rating badges and a "Fully Insured" line.
11. **Branded band** — full-bleed dark band with the wordmark logo + a hexagon/collage of real work
    photos ("Residential & Commercial"). Gives the page a strong brand moment.
12. **Audience / sectors grid** — "Serving All Sectors" — blue cards for each customer type
    (Educational Institutions, Residential Driveways, Places of Worship, Clinics & Hospitals,
    Commercial & Apartment Parking). Helps every visitor self-identify.
13. **Contact CTA** — multi-channel: "Send Us a Text", "Make Your Appointment", a LOUD phone number
    ("Call for Service: (984) 381-8149"), Schedule Online button, Google rating. Phone is king for trades.
14. **Brand-partner logo strip** — "We Proudly Work With:" — real supplier logos (Graco, SealMaster,
    Sherwin-Williams) in restrained grayscale. Borrowed credibility.
15. **Gold footer** — the brand-color footer band: columns (Commercial / Residential services, About,
    Areas Served, Office Hours with day-by-day times, Mission statement) + the wordmark logo. Use
    "8AM to 5PM" (the word "to"), never a dash.

## What to keep from the rest of the skill
- All §0 anti-slop rules, §0.7 honesty creed, the **no-dashes** ban (hours = "8AM to 5PM"), the
  humanizer pass on all copy, real icons (Iconify/Lucide/simple-icons), real photos saved into the
  project (§2.5 image pipeline — lead with a real photo of the actual work/crew), SEO suite, a11y,
  responsive (375 + 1440), and verify-with-screenshots-before-done.
- §5 color discipline (every color earns its place) and §5.5 psychology (pain→solution→result, trust
  stacking, one big promise, friction removal) — this genre is the PUREST application of §5.5.

## What to OVERRIDE from §0.5 for this genre
- **Light, not dark, by default.** Warm + clean beats moody. (Dark stays the default only for
  AI/SaaS/premium-tech brands.)
- **Hero = benefit headline + real work photo / before-after slider + the quote form**, NOT a live
  animated product demo or model marquee. There is no "product" to mock — the proof is the work.
- Swap "Models/integrations marquee" → "brand-partner / supplier logo strip".
- Swap "Pricing cards" → "Free estimate / quote form" as the conversion target (trades quote per job).
- Keep legal pages (`/privacy`, `/terms`) and a custom `not-found`; the core detail page becomes a
  **Services** page (and optionally per-service pages for local SEO).

## One-line trigger test
If the client is a real-world local service/trade (home or property services, visually-driven local
business) whose goal is **phone calls and quote requests from a nearby area** — build THIS genre:
light, warm, trust-stacked, quote-form-driven, real photos, friendly brand character. Not the dark
product page.
